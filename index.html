We care about our customers, and our main goal is to produce a high–quality burger. The production of our burgers is carried out in strict compliance with sanitary and quality standards. The technology of burger preparation includes the careful selection of ingredients, preparation of the patty, seasoning, grilling, assembling the burger, and packaging. We also take a very thoughtful approach to the selection of components for each burger. There are several important rules that we adhere to, and they will be discussed below.

Rule 1. Selection of buns

The bun is the base of the burger and plays a crucial role in texture and flavor. It should be fresh, soft on the inside, and have a slight crispiness on the outside. Popular choices include brioche for its buttery sweetness, potato buns for a denser texture, and whole grain buns for a nuttier, firmer bite. A well–chosen bun should complement the burger’s texture, absorbing juices without becoming soggy. Light toasting enhances both texture and taste.

Rule 2. Products

A great burger starts with great ingredients. The quality of the meat, vegetables, cheese, and sauces directly impacts the final taste. When choosing meat, a higher fat content ensures a juicy, flavorful patty. Fresh vegetables add crunch and brightness, balancing out the richness of the meat and cheese. The cheese should be selected based on its melting properties–classic choices like cheddar, Swiss, and American cheese work well because they melt evenly and contribute to a creamy texture. Sauces should complement, not overpower, the burger’s natural flavors, whether it’s a classic mayo–based spread, smoky barbecue sauce, or a tangy mustard blend.

Rule 3. Cooling

One of the biggest mistakes in burger preparation is handling the meat too much or cooking it while it's too warm. Ground meat should be kept cold until just before cooking to maintain its structure and prevent excessive shrinkage. Chilling the patties before they hit the grill or pan helps them hold their shape, keeping them juicy inside and perfectly seared outside. Similarly, fresh vegetables and sauces should be stored at the right temperature to retain their crispness and flavor, ensuring that every bite has a refreshing contrast to the warm, savory patty.

Rule 4. Texture and uniformity

A burger is not just about taste — it’s about texture and balance. The patty should be uniform in shape to ensure even cooking, and its size should match the bun to prevent an awkward eating experience. The best burgers achieve the perfect contrast between textures: a crispy, toasted bun, a tender yet structured patty, crunchy vegetables, and creamy cheese or sauces. Overloading the burger with too many ingredients can throw off the balance, making it difficult to eat and diminishing the enjoyment of each individual component.

Rule 5. Roasting

The way a patty is cooked determines much of its final flavor and texture. Proper searing creates a delicious crust while keeping the inside juicy. The best methods include grilling over an open flame for a smoky taste or using a cast–iron skillet for an evenly caramelized surface. Cooking the patty on medium–high heat helps achieve a golden–brown crust without drying out the inside. It’s also important to resist the urge to press down on the patty while it cooks — doing so releases essential juices, making the burger dry. A properly cooked patty should be firm yet slightly springy when pressed.

Rule 6. Compatibility

A well–made burger is more than just a collection of ingredients — it’s a carefully crafted combination of complementary flavors. Every component should work together to create depth and harmony. Spicy sauces can balance fatty meats, while pickled ingredients like onions or cucumbers add acidity to cut through the richness of cheese. The bun should subtly enhance the flavors without overshadowing them, while toppings should be thoughtfully selected to bring contrast without overwhelming the dish. When choosing ingredients, consider how they will interact in a single bite to create a well–rounded experience.

Rule 7. Supplements

Additional toppings can take a burger to the next level, but moderation is key. Ingredients like crispy bacon, fried eggs, caramelized onions, or sautéed mushrooms can add extra layers of flavor and texture, but too many elements can create an unbalanced, messy burger. Each topping should serve a purpose, whether it’s adding smokiness, umami, sweetness, or crunch. Instead of piling on every possible ingredient, focus on a few high–quality additions that will complement the base flavors of the burger.

Rule 8. Feeding and storage

The best way to enjoy a burger is fresh off the grill, while the ingredients are at their peak flavor and texture. If a burger needs to be stored or transported, wrapping it in parchment paper or foil helps retain heat without making the bun too soggy. However, burgers should not be left sitting for too long, as the juices from the patty and toppings can cause the bun to become overly soft, diminishing the overall experience. For the best results, always aim to serve burgers immediately after preparation, ensuring that each bite is hot, fresh, and perfectly balanced.
